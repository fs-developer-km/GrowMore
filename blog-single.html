How to Increase the Life of Your Refrigerator

  1. Admin
  2. 11 April 2025

Your refrigerator is one of the most important appliances in your home — it runs 24 hours a day, 7 days a week. With proper care and regular maintenance, you can significantly extend its lifespan and avoid costly repairs. Simple habits like cleaning the coils, checking door seals and maintaining the right temperature can make a huge difference in how long your refrigerator lasts and how efficiently it operates.

Most refrigerators have a lifespan of 10 to 15 years, but neglecting basic maintenance can reduce this drastically. Dust buildup on condenser coils forces the compressor to work harder, increasing energy consumption and wear. Regularly cleaning the coils, defrosting when needed and ensuring proper ventilation around the unit are small steps that go a long way in keeping your refrigerator running smoothly for years.

A well-maintained refrigerator not only lasts longer but also consumes less electricity, keeps your food fresher and saves you from unexpected breakdown costs. Regular servicing is the smartest investment you can make for your appliance.

Apart from cleaning, it is important to monitor the temperature settings of your refrigerator. The ideal temperature for the fridge compartment is between 3°C to 5°C and the freezer should be set at -18°C. Incorrect temperature settings can lead to food spoilage and put extra strain on the compressor, leading to early breakdown.

Top tips to keep your refrigerator in perfect condition

Following a regular maintenance routine can help you avoid major repair bills and keep your refrigerator performing at its best. Here are some easy and effective tips to increase the life of your refrigerator and ensure it runs efficiently every day.

  • Clean the condenser coils every 6 months to prevent dust buildup and improve cooling efficiency.
  • Check and replace worn door gaskets to ensure a proper seal and prevent cold air from escaping.
  • Avoid overloading the refrigerator as it restricts airflow and forces the compressor to work harder.
  • Defrost your refrigerator regularly if it does not have an auto-defrost function to maintain efficient operation.
  • Get a professional service done once a year to inspect the compressor, gas levels and overall performance.

Taking care of your refrigerator does not require much effort but the benefits are enormous. Regular cleaning, correct temperature settings and timely professional servicing can easily double the lifespan of your appliance.
